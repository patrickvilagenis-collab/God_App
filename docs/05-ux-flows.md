# UX Flows

Key journeys, written to be opinionated and buildable. Tone throughout: calm, warm,
unhurried, never clinical, never preachy. Bilingual (EN/ES) from the first screen.

## 1. First-time user — discovery → faith selection → first conversation

The first five minutes decide whether a guarded young seeker trusts us. Design for
**safety and low pressure**, not feature showcasing.

1. **Welcome (no wall).** A calm landing: one warm line ("A space to be heard, whoever
   you are, wherever you are on your journey."), language toggle (EN/ES) right away.
   *No forced signup to feel the tone* — let them peek/try one exchange if feasible.
2. **The promise, stated plainly.** Before anything personal: "This is private. No
   judgment. I'm a companion, not an authority — and not a therapist or doctor." Sets
   expectations and the boundary in one breath.
3. **Gentle entry question, not a form.** Instead of a cold "select your religion"
   dropdown, ask conversationally: "Where are you right now with faith?" with soft
   options: *Practicing · Returning · Curious / exploring · Not sure / just need
   someone.* This honors the "any stage" principle and routes the experience.
4. **Faith tradition selection (skippable / changeable).** Offer traditions as warm
   cards (Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Buddhism, Hinduism, Exploring…). Then optional
   depth ("Want to get more specific?" → denomination) and a strictness preference. Make
   clear: *you can skip this and change it anytime.* "Exploring/Not sure" → intro-to-
   faith on-ramp.
5. **Account creation, framed as privacy.** Now ask to create a private space — framed as
   "so your reflections stay yours." OAuth/email. Explain encryption in one human line.
6. **Companion intro + personalization.** Briefly meet the companion; optionally name it,
   pick a soothing voice or text-first, set warmth/brevity. Skippable.
7. **First conversation.** Open invitation: "What's on your heart today?" with obvious
   text + voice-note + photo affordances. The companion leads with presence, reflects,
   asks one gentle question, and only then (if it fits) offers a grounded thought.

**Principles:** progressive disclosure (don't front-load forms), every step skippable/
reversible, privacy and no-judgment surfaced early, conversation over configuration.

## 2. Returning user — home space

- A calm dashboard, not a busy feed: a warm greeting, "continue our conversation,"
  recent journal, a single gentle daily offering (passage/reflection in their tradition),
  and quick access to library and journal. No streaks, no guilt mechanics, no engagement
  bait.

## 3. A companionship conversation (the core loop)

1. User shares (text / voice note / photo / combination).
2. Companion: validates and holds space → asks/reflects → optionally offers a
   tradition-grounded thought with a real citation → invites continuation.
3. Response delivered as text and/or chosen voice (toggleable inline).
4. Optional: "Would you like to journal about this?" → one tap into a pre-seeded entry.
5. If crisis signals appear → safety flow (see safety doc) takes priority over everything.

## 4. Journaling

- Entry list (private), "New entry" (free-form or pick a guided prompt in their
  tradition), optional **"Ask for a reflection"** button (AI feedback is opt-in per
  entry, never automatic/graded). "Extra-private" mode available (no AI processing).

## 5. Library

- Browse by their tradition's text(s) + curated resources + intro-to-faith track. Full-
  text search. Clean reading view. From any passage: "Talk to my companion about this."

## 6. Settings (control = trust)

- Faith tradition & depth (change anytime) · Language · Voice (preset/cloned/off) ·
  Companion personalization (name, warmth, brevity) · Privacy (export data, delete data,
  manage/delete voice model, memory controls, training opt-in default OFF) · Notifications
  (gentle, off by default-aggressive) · Crisis resources.

## 7. Accessibility & inclusivity

- WCAG-minded: voice-first paths help low-literacy and low-vision users; captions/
  transcripts for audio; large-text and high-contrast support; simple language; works on
  low-end devices and modest connections (the audience isn't all on flagship phones).
